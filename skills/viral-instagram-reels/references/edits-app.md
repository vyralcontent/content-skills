# Edits app

Edits is Meta's free standalone video editor for Reels. It's the in-house
alternative to CapCut and the safer default for anyone who's been getting
CapCut watermarks into their exports. It is mobile-only at present and
publishes directly into Reels with deeper Insights integration than a
generic third-party editor.

It is not a hook generator, a viral-script tool, or a substitute for the
craft work in the rest of this skill. It's an editor. The reason it gets
its own reference here is that two specific Edits features actually move
distribution: no watermark on export, and tighter Insights wiring.

## When Edits helps

- **You've been getting CapCut watermarks through.** This is the most
  common case. Edits exports cleanly with no third-party watermark, which
  removes one of the biggest silent reach caps on Reels (see
  `originality-and-audio.md` for the watermark penalty).
- **You want template workflows tied to trending audio.** Edits surfaces
  templates that pair pre-cut beats with currently trending sounds. If the
  goal is to ride a sound trend, the in-app template flow saves time and
  keeps the audio attachment clean through publish.
- **You want Insights tied tighter to the edit decisions.** Edits passes
  metadata through to Reels Insights more cleanly than third-party
  exports. The retention curve and post-watch behaviour read against the
  cuts you made.
- **You're optimising for one platform.** Edits is built for Reels first.
  If you're not also cross-posting to TikTok or YouTube Shorts from the
  same export, the in-app flow is faster.

## What Edits doesn't fix

- **A weak hook.** Edits gives you more control over cuts and captions, but
  it does not rewrite a hook that opens on "hi guys." Hook work happens
  before you open any editor. (See `reels-hook.md`.)
- **A muddy topic.** Edits can put on-screen text on the first frame, but
  it can't make you choose the right keyword phrase. Caption and on-screen
  text decisions belong in the draft, not in the editor. (See
  `caption-and-search.md`.)
- **A non-shareable payoff.** Edits is not a content strategy. A Reel that
  earns no sends because the payoff isn't useful to a specific person
  won't be saved by a smoother edit. (See `sends-playbook.md`.)
- **Originality category violations.** A Reel that is mostly someone
  else's footage is still unoriginal in Edits. The watermark goes away
  but the substantial-edit bar still applies (see
  `originality-and-audio.md`).
- **Cross-platform optimisation.** Edits exports a Reels-shaped file. If
  you're also publishing to TikTok or Shorts, you may want to edit
  somewhere that gives you cleaner control over multiple aspect ratios.

## Practical workflow notes

- Edits is mobile-only at present. There is no desktop version. If your
  craft workflow lives on a laptop, Edits is a downgrade for editing
  itself. Use it for the export step or for the trending-audio template
  flow.
- The template library leans toward currently trending audio. Templates
  expire as audio trends saturate (see `originality-and-audio.md` on the
  trending audio window).
- Edits supports direct publish to Reels with caption, on-screen text, and
  the trial toggle in the same flow. You don't have to re-export and
  re-upload.
- Captions written in Edits feed straight into the Reel. Make sure the
  first line of the Edits caption is the keyword phrase, same as you
  would in the Instagram caption flow.

## When CapCut or another editor is still the right call

- You're already cross-posting and your CapCut workflow exports to
  TikTok-shape and Reels-shape from the same project, and you've fixed
  the watermark export setting. Re-tooling around Edits just for Reels
  may not be worth the friction.
- You're editing on desktop. CapCut and other desktop editors stay better
  than going mobile.
- The Edits template you want doesn't exist. Templates are a small slice
  of all possible Reel formats.

## Anti-patterns

- **Treating Edits as a virality tool.** It's an editor. The patterns in
  the rest of this skill still do the work.
- **Using Edits templates blindly.** A template tied to a current trending
  audio works for the first wave of users. By the time a template is
  obvious, the audio is saturated and the lift is gone. Treat templates
  as starting points, not as guaranteed hits.
- **Skipping the originality pass because Edits exported it.** A clean
  Edits export with no watermark can still be unoriginal under the
  guidelines if the underlying footage is someone else's clip. Edits
  doesn't change the substantial-edit bar.

## Quick decision

- Watermarked export from CapCut hitting Reels? Switch to Edits for the
  export step.
- Want to ride a trending audio with a clean template? Try Edits.
- Already have a working desktop edit workflow with no watermark? Stay
  put.
- Hook is weak? Don't open any editor yet. Fix the hook first.
