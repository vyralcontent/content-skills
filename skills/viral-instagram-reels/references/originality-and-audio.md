# Originality and audio

Meta's Original Content Guidelines are the structural rule that decides
whether your Reel is eligible for cold distribution at all. Accounts that
primarily post unoriginal content are excluded from recommendations to
non-followers. Followers can still see the posts in feed, but the cold-traffic
tap is closed. Originality is also the only lever in this skill that can flip
from "fine" to "shadow-capped" with no warning, so it deserves its own pass
on every Reel.

Audio is the second half of the same pass. The trending-audio lever still
moves cold reach, but only on accounts that can use it (Creator, not
Business) and only inside a short trend window.

## What counts as original

Meta defines original content as material you shot, designed, or
substantially edited. The bar is transformation, not novelty. A Reel using
licensed audio and your own footage is original. A Reel reposting someone
else's video with a watermark on top is not.

The following do not count as transformation on their own:

- Adding borders, frames, or letterboxing.
- Stamping a watermark on top.
- Speed changes (sped up or slowed down) with no other edit.
- Attribution overlays ("credit to @creator").
- Basic crops or zooms.

The following do count as substantial transformation:

- Adding your own voiceover or commentary throughout.
- Cutting and re-sequencing clips with new pacing.
- Adding original on-screen text that changes the meaning.
- Reaction format where your face and reactions take significant screen time.
- Translating, subtitling with original analysis, or restructuring into a
  new format.

If you're not sure whether a Reel qualifies, it probably doesn't. Treat
borderline cases as risky.

## The watermark penalty

Mosseri has explicitly said Reels with TikTok or CapCut watermarks get
reduced reach in Explore and recommendation surfaces. Your own brand
watermark is fine. Instagram clarified that own-logo watermarks don't trigger
the penalty.

Practical rules:

- Never publish a Reel with a TikTok watermark visible. Re-export the footage
  through any editor that doesn't stamp one (Instagram's Edits app does not).
- Never publish a CapCut watermark. The free CapCut export adds one by
  default. Remove it before posting or edit somewhere else.
- Your own brand logo, handle, or website on screen is safe.
- App UI in the corner of a screen-recording counts as a third-party
  watermark in practice. Crop it out or recreate the screen capture cleanly.

The penalty is silent. You won't see a warning. The Reel just doesn't get
distribution to non-followers. By the time you notice the pattern across
multiple Reels, you may already be in a recovery window.

## The rolling 30-day recovery window

Originality is judged over a rolling 30-day window of your account's posts.
Once most of your last 30 days qualify as original, you become eligible for
recommendations to non-followers again.

What this means in practice:

- One unoriginal Reel won't cap your account. A run of them will.
- Recovery is automatic but not instant. It takes posting original content
  consistently over the window for the account to come back into
  recommendations.
- The window resets day by day, not after a single corrective post. There's
  no "I posted one original Reel, I'm fixed" lever.
- New accounts don't get a grace period. The same rules apply from day one.

If an account suspects it's in a recovery window (sudden drop in non-follower
reach across multiple Reels, no policy notification, no obvious hook
problem), the move is to publish original Reels consistently for at least
30 days and stop reposting in the meantime. There's no appeals form.

## Creator vs Business audio access

Audio licensing is split by account type, and the difference matters a lot.

- **Creator accounts** get the full Instagram music library, including the
  popular trending songs that drive most audio-based distribution lifts.
- **Business accounts** are restricted to the commercial-use Sound Collection,
  which is a much smaller pool and largely excludes the songs driving current
  trends.

For any creator with commercial intent who still wants access to trending
audio, the working answer is to use a Creator account, not a Business
account. Switching is free and reversible.

Legal note: switching to Creator does not actually license you to use a
copyrighted song commercially. It just removes Instagram's in-app block. If
you're posting a Reel that ads a product, the trending song you can now
attach may still be a copyright risk for your brand. Most small creators
operate inside Instagram's de facto tolerance; large brands and ad
placements have a separate licensing problem.

If the Reel will be promoted as a paid ad or repurposed off-platform, default
to commercially licensed audio or original audio. Trending songs don't carry
through to ad versions cleanly.

## Trending audio windows

Trending audio still moves cold reach. The window is short.

- A typical audio trend runs about one to three weeks from breakout to
  saturation.
- The strongest lift is in the first five to seven days, before the sound
  is overused.
- A useful filter when picking an audio: it shows an upward trend arrow in
  the audio page, and the total uses are still relatively low (rough rule
  of thumb: fewer than around 30,000 uses).
- Many audio trends originate on TikTok and migrate to Reels with a one to
  two week lag. Cross-platform creators can sometimes catch a trend earlier
  on Reels by watching TikTok first.
- Audio trends that are obviously past peak (everyone has used the sound,
  multiple parodies exist, brands have jumped on it) no longer carry a
  distribution lift. Use the sound only if it actually fits the Reel, not
  for the lift.

## Audio attachment failure modes

A Reel can play with no audio if licensing fails silently:

- A Business account picking a trending song outside the commercial pool
  will see the audio strip on publish. The Reel plays muted for most viewers.
- Music rights vary by region. A sound that's available in one country may
  be blocked in another. International audiences may see the Reel muted.
- An audio added at edit time may be removed at publish if Instagram
  detects a copyright issue.

After publishing, check the Reel on a test non-creator account to confirm
audio is attached. If it's stripped, recut with a safer audio choice.

## Originality and audio pass checklist

Before publishing or trialling, confirm:

- No TikTok watermark visible anywhere in the frame.
- No CapCut watermark in the export.
- No third-party app UI in the corner of any screen recording.
- The Reel qualifies as substantially edited (your voice, your cuts, your
  on-screen text), not a repost with a wrapper.
- Audio choice fits the account type (Creator for trending, Sound Collection
  for Business).
- Trending audio is still inside its window (upward arrow, not yet saturated).
- The Reel was test-played on a non-creator account to confirm audio
  attached cleanly.

If any item fails, fix before publishing. The cost of one slipped item is
days of capped distribution.
