# Sound strategy on TikTok

Sound on TikTok is a metadata signal, a discovery channel, and a retention
lever at the same time. The choice between a trending track and an original
audio is a strategic decision, not a styling preference.

## Why sound matters more on TikTok

- **Audio is searchable IP.** Tapping a sound opens a feed of every video
  using it. Original audio that catches becomes a discovery path back to
  the originator.
- **Trending sounds carry their own seed signal.** The algorithm reads
  "video uses currently-trending audio" as a hint about the cohort to
  test it against.
- **Sound-on viewing is the default.** Unlike Reels (often muted) and
  Shorts (mixed), TikTok viewers typically play with sound. The audio is
  doing real work on retention.

## Trending vs original: the working balance

For serious accounts:

- **Trending audio for cold reach.** Use it when the goal is to land on
  FYP and gain new viewers.
- **Original audio for brand recognition and reusable IP.** Use it when
  the goal is to build a signature sound viewers associate with you, or
  when the audio itself is the content (voiceover, demo sound, signature
  catchphrase).

Many successful accounts run both: trending audio on cold-reach posts,
original audio on the format pieces.

## The 48–72 hour window

Trending sounds have a narrow useful life. Creators who adopt a sound in
the first 48–72 hours of it trending tend to capture the strongest reach
lift. By the time the sound is everywhere, the boost is gone and the
algorithm starts treating it as saturated.

How to spot sounds early:

1. **TikTok Creative Center sounds page.** Filter by "Breakout" and your
   region. Breakouts are sounds whose use is accelerating, not sounds that
   are already huge.
2. **The sounds icon on FYP videos.** When you see the same sound twice in
   one scrolling session, check the sound page. If it has under ~10k uses
   and is climbing, it is inside the window.
3. **Niche-specific Discord and creator chats.** Other creators in your
   space spot sounds before the public trend boards do.

Log spotted sounds with a ride-or-skip deadline. See
`assets/sound-tracker.md`.

## Account type is a binding constraint

This catches creators out routinely. The account type you chose at signup
determines the sounds you can legally use.

- **Creator account:** full licensed music library, including the trending
  pop and viral tracks. Required for serious creators who want trending
  sounds.
- **Business account:** limited to the Commercial Music Library (~150k
  royalty-free tracks) that lags real trends by days or weeks. Also loses
  eligibility for Creator Rewards.

For any creator with commercial intent who wants trending audio, the
answer is Creator account, not Business account. The "Business" label
suggests it is the right pick for selling; in practice it locks you out
of the strongest reach lever.

You can switch from Business to Creator in Settings → Account. It does
not reset your followers or analytics.

## When to use original audio on purpose

- **Voiceover-led formats.** Storytelling, education, GRWM-with-narration.
  Original is required because you need the spoken track to sit cleanly
  in front of music.
- **Demo content with diegetic sound.** ASMR, unboxing, product use. The
  real audio is the hook.
- **Signature catchphrase or repeated line.** Building a sound viewers
  associate with you specifically (Khaby Lame's silent gesture is the
  canonical case, no audio at all became the signature).
- **Brand content where you want the audio reusable.** If you want the
  sound to spread with other creators making derivative content, original
  is the only path.

## Sound choice and the hook

- **Trending sound should match the energy of the opening visual.** A
  dramatic drop on a soft demo reads as misaligned and loses the second-3
  hold.
- **The hook line should land before the sound's beat drop.** If the
  trending sound has an obvious peak, structure the script so the payoff
  lands on or just after it.
- **Avoid muted-first openings.** TikTok often autoplays with sound; a
  silent first half-second can register as a broken upload and drive
  swipe-away.

## Sound and the cross-platform problem

Trending TikTok audio rarely transfers cleanly to Reels or Shorts. If you
are cross-posting:

- **Re-export with platform-native audio.** Cross-posted with TikTok's
  trending track baked in, the video can hit copyright friction on Reels
  and lose the algorithmic boost on Shorts.
- **Or use original audio everywhere.** Removes the cross-platform audio
  problem entirely and builds reusable brand IP. This is the path for
  serious multi-platform creators.
