# Retention math by length

On TikTok, completion rate is the gate. Watch time and completion outweigh
likes. If the video does not clear the completion bar for its length tier,
distribution stalls regardless of how good the comments look. Pick a length
on purpose, then build to its math.

## Working benchmarks by length

These are working thresholds observed across multiple creator and platform
analyses. TikTok has not published an official "viral threshold." Treat
these as the bar, not a guarantee.

| Length     | Target completion | What it implies |
|------------|-------------------|-----------------|
| Under 15s  | >100%             | The video has to loop. Viewers replay before swiping. |
| 15–30s     | ~70%              | Most viewers watch to the end. The classic sweet spot. |
| 30–60s     | ~50%              | Half your seed cohort completes. Needs at least one mid-video re-hook. |
| 60s+       | ~40%              | Two retention resets and a payoff stack. Required for Creator Rewards. |

Below the gate for your tier, the algorithm tends to stop pushing. Above it,
you earn the next expansion.

## The 3-second cliff

The first three seconds decide whether anything else matters. Industry
analyses report:

- Videos holding 85%+ through second 3 tend to pull several times the total
  views of videos under 60%.
- Videos in the 70–85% band still pull roughly double.
- Videos under 60% rarely break out of the seed cohort.

Treat the multipliers as directional, not as TikTok-published metrics. The
mechanism is real: the algorithm reads the second-3 drop as the cleanest
signal that the hook landed.

Three things blow the cliff:

1. **A logo or intro animation in the opening frame.** Burns 1–2 seconds
   before the actual hook.
2. **"Hey guys, welcome back."** Adds nothing, signals creator-channel
   format on a platform that does not reward it.
3. **A setup sentence before the payoff.** "Today I'm going to talk about
   why coffee matters" loses to "Your coffee tastes worse because of this
   $400 thing."

See `tiktok-hooks.md` for the archetypes that survive the cliff.

## Loops and the under-15s tier

A video shorter than 15 seconds rarely "completes" in the way longer videos
do. To clear the bar, it has to loop. That means:

- The final frame should feel like the start. Same energy, same visual, or
  a line that resolves into the opening line.
- The audio should land cleanly at the loop point, not mid-syllable.
- Content density has to be high enough that the viewer is willing to
  rewatch to catch what they missed the first time.

Looped-end-to-beginning videos tend to average 15–30% higher completion
than non-looping equivalents of the same length.

## Rewatches and the save signal

A 20–30% rewatch rate on short clips reads as a strong quality signal.
Saves read as future intent ("I will need this later") and are weighted
especially heavily for educational and utility content. Both compound:
saves drive rewatches when the viewer comes back, and rewatches drive the
completion math the algorithm reads first.

Practical levers:

- **Build for "send this to a friend" or "save this for later."** A list of
  three things beats a single tip for both metrics.
- **Front-load value.** Survive the steep early drop-off by putting your
  second-strongest moment in seconds 3–8.
- **Tease the payoff in the hook.** A viewer who knows a payoff is coming
  is willing to sit through the middle.

## The short-for-reach vs 60s-for-pay trade-off

This is a real strategic choice, not a style preference.

- **21–34s clips** still produce the highest raw completion rates. Roughly
  62% completion on average in creator analyses, vs ~48% for 60s+. This is
  the tier for cold reach and follower growth.
- **60s+ clips** are the only tier eligible for Creator Rewards monetization.
  The math is harder (you need ~40% completion on a longer runway), but
  paid views land here.

The serious-account answer is usually: build the format short for reach,
then produce a longer cut of the strongest hits for monetization. Do not
publish the same clip in both lengths inside a few days.

## Diagnosing a draft against the math

When a user hands you a script or a posted video that underperformed:

1. **Time it out loud.** Length tier sets the bar. A 47-second cut needs
   ~50% completion, which means at least one re-hook around second 25.
2. **Find the second-3 cliff.** Read the first three seconds out loud.
   Does the hook land before second 2? If not, that is the failure mode.
3. **Find the sag.** Mark the point in the script where the energy
   plateaus. That is where the watcher swipes. Insert a re-hook line
   ("but here is the part nobody expects") and a visual reset.
4. **Check the loop or the payoff.** Under 15s, the final frame must loop.
   Over 30s, the payoff must be visible (a result, a number, a reveal),
   not just stated.
