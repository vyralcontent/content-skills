# Native features

TikTok rewards content that uses its own features. Duets, stitches,
shoppable links, LIVE highlights, and the Symphony AI suite all sit
behind native UI that signals to the algorithm "this is a TikTok-shaped
video, not a cross-post." Use them when they earn their place, skip when
they do not.

## Duets

A duet pairs your video side-by-side with another creator's. The duet
shows up on the original video's page too, which gives you a discovery
channel into someone else's audience.

When duets help reach:

- **Reacting to a video with a strong reach trajectory.** You inherit
  some of its momentum, especially in the first 24 hours after the
  original posts.
- **Adding context, expertise, or a contrarian take.** A reaction that
  says something new beats a silent watch-along.
- **Niche-aligned content.** The original's audience overlaps yours, so
  the seed cohort the duet gets tested against is already warm.

When duets fail:

- **Reacting to a viral video outside your niche** for borrowed reach.
  The algorithm reads the misalignment and the seed cohort underperforms.
- **Silent reactions.** Add a line, an expression, a piece of context.
  A mute face on the right side of the screen rarely holds the cliff.
- **Reacting to a video without permission settings.** Some creators
  disable duets; check before you plan around it.

## Stitches

A stitch lets you clip 1–5 seconds of another creator's video and append
your own. The mechanic is more flexible than duets because the source
clip plays first and primes the viewer for your response.

Stitch shapes that work:

- **"You asked, I answered."** Clip a question from a comment-reply
  video, then deliver the answer.
- **"Here is what they got wrong."** Clip a claim, then disprove it
  with evidence. Drives saves and comments.
- **"And then this happened."** Clip a setup, then deliver an unexpected
  payoff.

Treat the clipped section as your hook. The viewer's second-3 cliff fires
on the source clip, not on your part. Pick a tight 2–3 second source
section that lands a clear question, claim, or cliffhanger.

## Shoppable video

Shoppable video adds product cards directly inside the post. Tap routes
to the product page in TikTok Shop or the creator's affiliate link.
Available to creators in the Affiliate program and to TikTok Shop sellers.

What works on shoppable video:

- **Problem-first framing.** Hook on the problem, product appears as the
  solution. See `shop-and-monetization.md`.
- **Product in frame by second 6.** TikTok's own ads playbook puts the
  product on screen inside the first six seconds for the strongest
  brand-affinity lift.
- **Real-use demo.** Watch the product solve the problem. Not a static
  product shot.
- **Soft close.** The link is the close. The verbal CTA can be light
  ("link's in the cart") because the visual UI is doing the work.

What fails:

- **Direct-response without trust.** A shoppable video that opens with
  "buy this" performs worse than the same product wrapped in a problem
  story. The surface reads ad-shaped and the swipe-away fires early.
- **Cross-posted shoppable content from a deck.** Polished ad creative
  rarely lands. Native-feeling, slightly raw beats high-production for
  shoppable.

## LIVE shopping and LIVE highlights

LIVE shopping is a separate format with its own mechanics, but the
relevant feature for short-form creators is **LIVE highlights**: TikTok
auto-clips compelling LIVE moments into shoppable videos that can run
on FYP after the stream ends.

Practical use:

- **Treat the LIVE itself as raw material.** Run a 30–60 minute LIVE
  with clear demo segments, knowing the highlights will become posts.
- **Mark the demo moments deliberately.** A clean product-in-frame
  segment with a strong line is the auto-highlight candidate.
- **Check Seller Center after the stream** for what got auto-clipped
  and post-edit if needed.

## Symphony AI tools

TikTok's Symphony AI suite covers Image-to-Video, Text-to-Video, Showcase
Products, AI Dubbing, and Digital Avatars. Available to advertisers and
to individual creators through the Symphony Creative Studio.

When to use Symphony:

- **AI Dubbing for cross-language reach.** Original English audio dubbed
  to other languages widens the seed cohort without re-shooting.
- **Image-to-Video for product B-roll.** Quick variants of product shots
  for the demo middle of a script, when you do not have time to film
  every angle.
- **Showcase Products for catalog-driven content.** Useful for TikTok
  Shop sellers with many SKUs who need fast variant content.

When to skip:

- **Realistic AI-generated people, places, or events.** Triggers the AI
  disclosure requirement and reads as low-effort to many viewers. See
  `anti-patterns.md`.
- **AI-generated hooks.** The hook is the most viewer-sensitive part of
  the video; AI voice or AI face in the first three seconds tends to
  collapse the second-3 hold.
- **Replacing the creator's face entirely.** The format-IP that drives
  parasocial bond depends on a real human in frame. Avatar-only formats
  rarely break out.

Disclosed AI content is not systematically demoted by the algorithm. The
underperformance comes from creative quality and viewer behavior, not
the label itself.

## Captions, on-screen text, hashtags

These are not features in the duet/stitch sense, but they are the metadata
surface the algorithm reads most directly.

- **First sentence of the caption** does the heaviest lifting for both
  algorithmic indexing and the user's "read more" decision. Captions can
  run up to ~4,000 characters; only the first ~100–150 show above the
  fold. Front-load.
- **On-screen text** is indexed and searchable. Use it to restate the
  hook in 3–5 words and to mark key beats. Reinforce the audio, do not
  duplicate every word.
- **Hashtags** are 3–5 specific tags, not 20 broad ones. See
  `anti-patterns.md`.

for what to avoid, `shop-and-monetization.md` for the shoppable workflow.
