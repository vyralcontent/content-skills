# Shorts to long-form funnel

The click from a Short into a long-form video on the same channel is the most
valuable signal a Short can earn. YouTube weights it heavily because it
proves the Short converted a feed-scroller into a watcher, which is the
hardest conversion on the platform. Because the Shorts and long-form ranking
lanes run separately, this conversion no longer happens by accident. You
have to bridge it explicitly.

## Why this conversion matters more than subs

The math, in rough terms:

- A 1M-view Short tends to add somewhere in the range of 500 to 5,000
  subscribers (roughly 0.05 to 0.5%).
- A 1M-view long-form video tends to add 20,000 to 50,000 subscribers
  (roughly 2 to 5%).
- Shorts RPM tends to sit in the $0.03 to $0.10 per 1,000 views range. Long-
  form RPM in the same niches tends to sit at $3 to $15.

A Shorts-acquired subscriber who never clicks through to long-form is worth
very little. A Shorts viewer who clicks through to a long-form once is worth
roughly 30 to 100x more in revenue terms, and signals to the algorithm that
your Shorts and long-form belong together. Channels that run both formats
and bridge them well tend to grow subscribers roughly 3x faster and total
watch time roughly 2.5x faster than single-format channels in year one. The
multiplier is directional, not precise, and it requires the bridge to exist.

## The four places you can put the bridge

1. **In-video callout.** A spoken line plus on-screen text inside the Short,
   pointing at the long-form. The strongest pattern is naming the specific
   long-form ("full breakdown is the [topic] video on my channel"), not a
   generic "subscribe for more." It works because it gives the viewer a
   concrete next action tied to what they just watched.
2. **Pinned comment.** The first comment on the Short, pinned, with the
   direct link to the long-form. Short caption above the link explaining
   what's there. This catches viewers who finished the Short and started
   reading comments, which is a high-intent moment.
3. **End card / final frame.** The last 1 to 2 seconds of the Short, with a
   visible label pointing at the long-form. This pairs naturally with a loop
   design: end on the callout, loop back to the hook.
4. **Channel trailer and Shorts tab consistency.** When a Short pops,
   viewers tap the channel name and land on a Shorts-first view of the
   channel page. If your Shorts tab is topically consistent and your trailer
   is the right long-form, the conversion to long-form watch tends to lift
   measurably. If the Shorts tab is a mix of unrelated topics, the click
   tanks.

The bridge works best when at least three of these four fire at once on the
same Short.

## When to add a long-form to receive the Short

Sometimes the right answer is not to write the bridge but to write the long-
form first. Use this test:

- Does a long-form on the exact topic of the Short already exist on this
  channel? If yes, bridge to it.
- If no, is the topic broad enough that a long-form is worth making, or is
  this a one-off Short with no obvious long-form companion? If the topic is
  worth a long-form, write the Short to set up the long-form (a teaser, a
  cliffhanger, the surprising fact only) and ship the long-form first, then
  the Short.
- If the topic is genuinely one-off, skip the funnel CTA on this Short and
  let it earn reach and shares.

A Short with no bridge isn't broken. It just gives up the most valuable
signal on the platform.

## Topical consistency on the Shorts tab

The Shorts tab is now a real conversion surface, not a dumping ground.
Practical rules:

- Group Shorts by topic in batches. If the channel covers two topics, run
  one topic for a week, then switch, so the top of the Shorts tab is always
  thematically tight.
- Pin two or three of the strongest Shorts on the channel that match the
  long-form library. Pinned Shorts sit above chronological ones.
- Avoid posting off-brand Shorts immediately after a Short you expect to
  pop. The tap-through audience will see the next-most-recent Short first.

## Writing the in-video callout

The line should do three things:

1. Reference the Short's topic. ("If you want the full routine...")
2. Name the destination concretely. ("...the 12-minute version is the [exact
   title] video on my channel.")
3. Land before the loop, not in it. The callout belongs in the last 5
   seconds, with on-screen text reinforcing the spoken line.

Avoid "go subscribe" as the callout. The subscribe button on the Shorts
player sits at the bottom of the screen and accidental taps during swiping
are common, which means the actual sub action tends to fire whether you ask
for it or not. Asking for it costs you retention without lifting it.

## Writing the pinned comment

Keep it short and high-intent. A template:

> The full [topic] video is here, with [one specific extra not in the
> Short]: [link]

The "one specific extra" is the lever. "Same as the Short but longer" gets
no clicks. "The exact products I used, with timestamps" earns the click.

## Measuring whether the bridge works

In Studio, check the Short's traffic source for the long-form. The "External
and other" / "From a Short" attribution shows clicks from the Short itself.
Track these per-Short, not in aggregate, because one or two Shorts tend to
do most of the long-form sending. Double down on the patterns that send,
drop the ones that don't.
