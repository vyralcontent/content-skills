# Shorts search and shelf

The vertical Shorts Feed is the loudest surface, but it isn't the only one.
Two other surfaces matter for distribution: the Shorts shelf on the home
feed, and Shorts inside YouTube search. The optimization moves are
different for each.

## The Shorts shelf on the home feed

After the home feed redesign, roughly 80% of available home-feed slots are
allocated to Shorts. Long-form rows dropped from around 6 thumbnails down
to about 2, and a large Shorts row sits above or between them. Most
creators saw browse-traffic Shorts views rise after the change.

Practical implications:

- The Shorts shelf surfaces Shorts based on the viewer's home-feed signals,
  not on the Shorts Feed swipe behavior. So a Short can underperform on the
  Shorts Feed and still earn browse-traffic views from the shelf.
- Thumbnail-style first frame matters here. The shelf shows the first
  frame of the Short as a still. A blurry, low-contrast, or text-heavy
  first frame loses to a clean visual hook.
- Title shows under the shelf thumbnail in some layouts. Treat the title
  as a real entry point, not just an internal label. Concrete is better
  than clever.

How to optimize for the shelf:

1. Make the first frame work as a still image. Crop, contrast, and
   composition should hold up frozen, not just in motion.
2. Write titles that read like search-intent matches for what the Short
   delivers. Vague titles do worse on the shelf than specific ones.
3. Keep the Shorts tab on the channel page topically consistent. The shelf
   tends to surface Shorts from channels that the viewer's home feed
   already trusts.

## YouTube search for Shorts

Search has a dedicated Shorts filter, and the indexing behavior is
different from long-form. For Shorts, YouTube indexes:

- The title.
- The description.
- The spoken transcript (from YouTube's automated transcription).
- On-screen text (from automated OCR on the burned-in captions).

That last one matters. A Short with strong on-screen captions tends to
rank for queries that the spoken audio alone would miss. This is part of
why captions are not optional on Shorts: they do double duty as a search
signal.

How to optimize Shorts for search:

1. **Write the title like a search query.** "How to wash a cast-iron
   skillet" beats "The cast iron mistake everyone makes" if you want
   evergreen search traffic. Search-friendly titles can coexist with
   strong on-screen hooks.
2. **Use the description.** Two or three sentences with the search-intent
   keywords for what the Short answers. Not a wall of hashtags.
3. **Say the keywords out loud** in the voiceover. The transcript is
   indexed. A Short about cast-iron care should contain the phrase "cast
   iron" spoken clearly, not just implied.
4. **Burn the keywords into the on-screen text** for the same reason. OCR
   pulls them in as ranking signal.

Search-driven Shorts have a different shape than feed-driven Shorts. They
tend to be evergreen, answer a specific question, and accumulate views
over months rather than spiking in 72 hours. A library of search-driven
Shorts compounds into a real long-tail traffic source for the channel.

## Choosing the surface to optimize for

Most Shorts cannot be optimized for all three surfaces at once. Pick one as
the primary target:

- **Shorts Feed** is right for high-volume topical content, trends, and
  anything where reach in the first 72 hours is the goal. Optimize VVSA
  and loops.
- **Shelf** is right for channel-trust content. Optimize first-frame
  thumbnail quality and topical consistency.
- **Search** is right for evergreen how-to and answer-style Shorts.
  Optimize title, description, transcript keywords, and on-screen text.

A balanced upload schedule tends to mix all three: most of the volume goes
to Feed-optimized Shorts, with a steady drip of search-optimized Shorts
that compound over time, and shelf-friendly first frames as a baseline
quality bar across both.

## What does not work the same as on long-form

- **Tags.** Tags barely move ranking on Shorts. Do not waste effort here.
- **Thumbnails.** You cannot upload a custom thumbnail for a Short and
  have it show in the Shorts Feed (custom thumbnails only show on the
  Shorts tab and channel page). The first frame of the video is what the
  Feed and shelf use. Plan the first frame as if it were the thumbnail.
- **Cards and end screens.** Long-form cards do not appear on Shorts. The
  bridge to long-form is the in-video callout, the pinned comment, and
  the final-frame label, not a card overlay. See
  `shorts-to-longform-funnel.md`.
