# Content matrix

Justin Welsh's 2D grid, applied. Put 5 to 10 sub-topics from your
pillars down the left. The styles across the top come pre-filled. Each
cell is one idea. A filled 5 x 8 matrix is 40 ideas in one sitting, and
each cell can produce three to four pieces of derivative content.

Don't overthink any single cell. Generate fast; filter later with
`references/idea-evaluation.md`.

```
STYLES (across)
  A. Actionable how-to (ultra-specific)
  B. Contrarian take (defensible position against consensus)
  C. Cost confession (numbers, time, mistakes)
  D. Before / after (transformation, visual)
  E. Tier list (rank or compare)
  F. Beginner-mistake call-out (correct a common error)
  G. Behind-the-scenes (process or workflow)
  H. Listicle (3 to 7 specific things)
```

Filling rules:

- One specific idea per cell. "Beginner mistakes with X" is too vague.
  "The 3-second tamp check that fixed my channeling" is a cell.
- Leave cells blank if no real idea lands. Forced cells produce
  generic videos.
- Aim for 60 to 80% of cells filled. Pushing for 100% tends to
  produce filler.
- Don't filter while generating. Cut weak cells later in a separate
  pass with `references/idea-evaluation.md`.

Add more sub-topic rows or swap styles as the niche demands. Beauty
might want "ingredient deep-dive" as a style. Finance might want
"prediction." The grid scales.
