# Pillars

Pillars are 3 to 5 recurring topic categories that define what an account
posts about. They are the foundation every other ideation system in this
skill builds on. Fewer than 3 reads as repetitive. More than 5 dilutes the
niche and confuses both the audience and the algorithm about what to expect.

Pick pillars before generating ideas. If the user has none, draft them
first. If they have more than five, suggest collapsing before doing
anything else.

## What a pillar actually is

A pillar is a topic the account commits to covering regularly. It sits
above any single video. It tends to answer: "if a new follower scrolled
your last 30 posts, what would they say this account is about?"

Good pillars share three traits:

- **Audience-shaped, not creator-shaped.** "Things I'm into" is a hobby.
  "Decisions a first-time home buyer has to make" is a pillar. The pillar
  describes the viewer's problem space, not the creator's interests.
- **Narrow enough to recur.** A pillar you can only post about once a
  month is too broad. If you can produce 20+ angles inside it without
  reaching, the scope is right.
- **Distinct from the others.** If two pillars share most of their angles,
  they're one pillar. Collapse them.

## How to draft pillars from a niche

Walk the user through this in five minutes, not an hour.

1. **State the niche in one sentence.** "I make videos about home
   espresso for people who own a machine under $1,500." Force concrete
   audience and scope.
2. **List 8 to 12 candidate buckets.** Brainstorm wide, no judgement.
   Gear, technique, recipes, mistakes, brands, taste, water chemistry,
   tools, maintenance, history, café culture, etc.
3. **Cluster.** Group candidates that share an audience question. "Gear"
   and "buying decisions" often collapse. "Mistakes" and "technique"
   often collapse.
4. **Cut to 3 to 5.** Keep the clusters where the user has both taste
   and lived material. A pillar with no personal angle dies after a month.
5. **Sanity-check overlap.** Each pillar should have a clearly different
   center of gravity. If two pillars produce the same 10 video ideas,
   merge them.

The `assets/pillar-worksheet.md` template walks the user through this on
the page.

## The over-niching failure mode

Symptoms: idea drought after roughly 3 months, engagement falls across
multiple videos at once (not just one bad post), the creator feels like
they've "said everything." A common diagnostic test: ask the user to
brainstorm 50 ideas inside the niche in 20 minutes. If they can't get
past 15 without reaching, the niche is too tight to sustain a year of
posting.

Fix: widen one pillar at a time, driven by audience signal. Read
comments and DMs from the last month. The asks that recur but don't fit
any current pillar are the candidates. Add one. Don't blow up the niche.

## The under-niching failure mode

Symptoms: no consistent audience expectation, the algorithm can't decide
who to serve the account to, engagement is flat across every post
regardless of quality. The account is "interesting" but not memorable.

Fix: pick the two highest-performing posts of the last 90 days. What do
they share? Whatever the answer is, that's the real pillar the account
has been working in by accident. Cut the rest, or move it to a side
account.

## Pivoting without tearing down the niche

The healthy pivot is additive, not destructive.

- **Let audience signal lead.** Analytics showing unexpected interest in
  one sub-topic, comments asking for more of one thing, DMs about a
  recurring question. That's the seed of a pillar shift.
- **Widen one pillar at a time.** Don't replace three pillars at once.
  Add one, watch performance for 4 to 6 weeks, then prune the weakest
  original pillar if needed.
- **Keep the umbrella identity stable.** Even when pillars shift, the
  one-sentence niche from step 1 above should mostly hold. If it doesn't,
  it's not a pivot, it's a new account.

## When the user pushes back on pillars

Some creators argue against pillars entirely (Dan Koe's "anti-niche"
position). It can work, but only with a strong premise (Jay Clouse's
"spiky point of view") that does the work pillars would otherwise do.
For most creators, 3 to 5 pillars under one umbrella identity is the
safer middle. Recommend the premise route only if the user already has
a clearly defensible point of view.

## What to do once pillars are set

Pillars are an input to the ideation engines, not the engine itself.
Move to `frameworks.md` to pick the engine that fits the brief, then run
it across the pillars to produce ideas at volume.
