# Evergreen vs trend

Every account runs a mix of two kinds of content. Evergreen compounds
(search, library, repurposing). Trend amplifies short-term and expires.
The mix matters because skewing too far either way kills the account in
different ways.

## The 80/20 default

The widely-reported baseline among solo creators is roughly 80%
evergreen, 20% trending. Evergreen builds the library and the
compounding asset base. Trend creates the spikes that pull in new
viewers, who then find the evergreen catalog.

Adjust to niche velocity:

- **Fast-moving niches** (tech, finance, news, AI tooling) push toward
  60/40 or 70/30. The audience expects timely takes; pure evergreen
  reads as out of touch.
- **B2B and slower service niches** push toward 90/10. Trends rarely
  apply, and chasing them dilutes credibility.
- **Lifestyle and hobby niches** (cooking, fitness, beauty, gaming) sit
  near the 80/20 default. Some trends apply, most don't.

Test the user's niche before prescribing a ratio. A user who posts
about retirement planning needs a different mix than one who posts
about live-service video games. Ask, don't assume.

## Why evergreen wins on a long enough timeline

A trend video earns spike reach for a week then dies. An evergreen
video earns smaller reach in week one and continues to surface in
search, recommendation, and your own repurposing pipeline for months
or years. The math compounds.

A 70/30 evergreen-leaning mix tends to outperform a trend-heavy mix
over 6 to 12 months on total reach, because the evergreen library
keeps surfacing while the trend videos go cold. Treat that as
directional, not a guarantee.

The other quiet advantage of evergreen: it's the source material for
the repurposing engine. A trend video is hard to re-angle in 6 months
because the trend is dead. An evergreen video re-angles cleanly with a
new hook. See `repurposing.md` for how this becomes the lowest-effort
new-video source.

## The 48-hour trend window on TikTok

TikTok specifically can launch a trend into peak reach in under 48
hours. The practical rule across creator-strategy writing: jump on a
trend within 48 hours of first noticing it, or skip it.

The trend lifecycle for creator opportunity:

- **Days 1 to 3.** Massive opportunity, low competition. Early movers
  get disproportionate reach because the algorithm is still figuring out
  the trend's distribution.
- **Days 4 to 7.** Peak popularity, high competition. Late movers get
  buried unless their version of the trend is genuinely better.
- **Day 8+.** Move on. Posting the trend at this point reads as late.

If the user notices a trend on day 5, the answer is usually "skip it,
not worth the production time." This is a hard rule for most creators
who feel pressure to participate.

Short-form videos in the 15 to 30 second range tend to generate the
highest engagement for trend formats, because sound-driven and
visual-format trends compress best at that length.

## When evergreen becomes the trend (the seed-and-wait pattern)

The most efficient evergreen-to-trend path: seed a strong evergreen
format (transformation, list, contrast, before/after, tier list) and
re-post variants with whatever sound or format is trending that week.
The format is yours; the sound is borrowed.

This pulls trend-velocity reach into an evergreen asset. The trend
expires; the format you built keeps shipping with whatever sound comes
next.

The single highest-leverage move in this pattern: re-upload an old
evergreen winner during a related trend cycle. The asset already
exists. You change only the hook and the sound. Production cost is
near zero. Reach can be material.

## Diagnosing the mix on a real account

Pull the user's last 30 posts. Categorize each as evergreen or
trend. Look at:

- **Ratio.** Is it close to the recommended ratio for the niche? If
  not, name the gap.
- **Performance by category.** Are the evergreen videos compounding
  (still getting views weeks later)? Are the trend videos earning
  spike reach, or just adding noise?
- **The dead middle.** Watch for videos that are neither evergreen nor
  riding a current trend. These are the "I had to post something"
  posts, and they tend to drag the account's signal down. Cut them
  from the rotation if the audience isn't asking for them.

## The trap to avoid: chasing every trend

Some creators try to participate in every trend that crosses their
feed. The cost: every trend post is one less evergreen post that
would have compounded. A trend that takes 4 hours to produce and
earns spike reach for 4 days has burned the budget for an evergreen
video that would have earned compounding reach for 12 months.

Filter rule: a trend is worth participating in if (a) it's inside 48
hours of you first noticing it, and (b) you can adapt it cleanly to
your niche without losing what made the trend work in the first place.
Either condition failing kills the post.

## The trap to avoid: refusing every trend

The opposite mistake. Pure evergreen accounts tend to grow slowly
because they don't get the spike events that pull new viewers into the
catalog. Trends are the discovery engine; evergreen is the retention
engine. Need both.

A useful default: hold a budget of 1 to 2 trend videos a week, no
more, no less. If no trend fits the niche that week, skip it. Don't
force a trend post into a slot just because the calendar said "trend."
